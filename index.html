GST Rules & Help Guide

Understanding GSTR-3B filing, offset rules and ITC utilization

⚖️ GST Offset Rules
  1. IGST ITC → IGST liability first
  2. Remaining IGST ITC → CGST liability
  3. Remaining IGST ITC → SGST liability
  4. CGST ITC → CGST liability first
  5. Remaining CGST ITC → IGST liability
  6. SGST ITC → SGST liability first
  7. Remaining SGST ITC → IGST liability
  8. Any remaining liability → Cash Ledger (head-wise)
🏦 ITC Utilization Rules
  • ITC on capital goods may be availed over 5 years
  • ITC blocked on personal-use purchases (Sec 17(5))
  • ITC must be claimed within prescribed time limits
  • Excess ITC carries forward to next period
  • Eligible for refund under Section 54 of CGST Act
  • 2A/2B reconciliation required for ITC claims
🔄 RCM Rules
  • Applies under Sections 9(3) & 9(4) of CGST Act
  • Must be paid only via Electronic Cash Ledger
  • ITC on RCM available only after cash payment
  • Existing ITC cannot directly offset RCM
  • Declared in Table 3.1(d) of GSTR-3B
  • Late payment attracts 18% per annum interest
💵 Cash Ledger Usage
  • Used only when ITC is insufficient for liability
  • IGST cash → IGST liability only
  • CGST cash → CGST liability only
  • SGST cash → SGST liability only
  • Unutilized balance eligible for refund
  • Balance reflects amounts deposited in GST portal
💳 Credit Ledger
  • Represents accumulated ITC from all purchases
  • Used automatically per GST offset order rules
  • Balance carries forward to subsequent months
  • Cannot pay interest, TDS, TCS or penalties
  • Excess balance eligible for refund under Sec 54
  • Debit/credit entries auto-posted on filing
📅 GSTR-3B Filing Notes
  • Monthly self-declaration return under GST
  • Due date: 20th of next month (standard taxpayer)
  • Late fee: ₹50/day; ₹20/day for nil returns
  • Interest: 18% p.a. on delayed tax payment
  • Cannot be revised — use Form DRC-03 if needed
  • Always verify figures with your CA before filing
